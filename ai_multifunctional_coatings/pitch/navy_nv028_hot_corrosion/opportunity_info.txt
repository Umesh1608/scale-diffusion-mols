TOPIC SUMMARY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

NV028 seeks ICME (Integrated Computational Materials Engineering) approaches
to develop overlay and bond coatings with improved resistance to hot corrosion
in Navy gas turbine engines operating in marine environments.

Key technical elements from the topic:
  - Type I hot corrosion (850-950C): molten Na2SO4 fluxing of protective
    oxide scales, leading to catastrophic sulfidation
  - Type II hot corrosion (650-800C): eutectic salt mixtures causing
    localized pitting attack on MCrAlY coatings
  - Marine environment challenges: sea salt ingestion, sulfur in fuel,
    combined salt + temperature cycling
  - Current MCrAlY (M = Ni, Co, NiCo) bond coats degrade unacceptably
    in aggressive marine salt environments
  - Topic explicitly calls for ICME methodologies — computational
    approaches to screen and optimize coating compositions
  - Interest in novel compositions beyond incremental modifications
    to existing MCrAlY systems

Target applications:
  - Shipboard gas turbines: LM2500, LM2500+, MT30
  - Aircraft engines operating in marine/littoral environments
  - TBC bond coats and overlay coatings for hot-section components
    (blades, vanes, combustor liners)

TECHNOLOGY MAPPING: Core AI Platform → NV028
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Our AI-driven multifunctional coating platform maps to this topic as follows:

1. GNN PROPERTY PREDICTION → HOT CORROSION RESISTANCE
   - Base capability: Multitask GNN predicting coating properties from
     molecular/atomic graphs
   - Adaptation: Train on MCrAlY composition-property data; predict oxide
     formation energy, sulfidation susceptibility, Al/Cr activity, CTE,
     and beta-NiAl phase fraction
   - Transfer learning from MACE-MP foundation model enables training with
     limited MCrAlY-specific data (<500 compositions)

2. GENERATIVE DESIGN → NOVEL COATING COMPOSITIONS
   - Base capability: Conditional VAE generating novel molecular structures
   - Adaptation: Operate on compositional vectors rather than molecular
     graphs; generate MCrAlY-family compositions conditioned on target
     hot corrosion resistance, phase stability, and CTE match
   - Fragment-based approach: matrix (Ni/Co/NiCo), oxide formers (Cr, Al),
     reactive elements (Y, Hf, Si), strengtheners (Ta, Re, W)

3. MULTI-OBJECTIVE BAYESIAN OPTIMIZATION → PARETO-OPTIMAL COMPOSITIONS
   - Base capability: BoTorch qNEHVI for multi-objective optimization
   - Adaptation: Optimize hot corrosion resistance vs. oxidation resistance
     vs. thermomechanical compatibility vs. cost
   - Constraints: phase stability (no TCP phases, sufficient beta-NiAl),
     manufacturability (APS/EB-PVD/HVOF compatible)

4. CALPHAD INTEGRATION → PHYSICS-BASED VALIDATION
   - Additional capability specific to this topic: Thermo-Calc with
     TCNI/MOBNI databases for phase equilibria and diffusion simulations
   - Provides ground-truth validation of GNN predictions
   - DICTRA simulations for coating lifetime prediction

KEY DIFFERENTIATOR:
The topic explicitly requests ICME approaches. Our platform provides a
complete ICME pipeline: AI-driven exploration (generative model + MOBO)
with physics-based grounding (GNN surrogates trained on DFT + CALPHAD
validation). This is not just a database search or conventional CALPHAD
study — it is a closed-loop discovery engine that generates and evaluates
compositions beyond human intuition.
