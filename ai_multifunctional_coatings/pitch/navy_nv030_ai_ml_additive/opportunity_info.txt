TOPIC DESCRIPTION SUMMARY
================================================================================

NV030 seeks AI/ML solutions to optimize additive manufacturing (AM) processes
for depositing protective coatings on Navy platforms. Key areas of interest:

  - Process parameter optimization for coating quality (porosity, adhesion,
    microhardness, oxide content)
  - Physics-informed digital twins for spray/deposition processes (APS, HVOF,
    cold spray, LPPS, directed energy deposition)
  - In-situ monitoring and real-time process control using sensor data
    (particle diagnostics, pyrometry, acoustic emission)
  - Defect prediction and prevention during coating buildup
  - Surrogate models that accelerate virtual experimentation
  - Closed-loop optimization that reduces experimental campaigns

Navy applications include:
  - Thermal barrier coatings for gas turbine engines
  - Wear-resistant coatings for hydraulic and mechanical components
  - Corrosion-resistant coatings for shipboard and hull structures
  - Cold spray repair of aluminum and magnesium components

TECHNOLOGY MAPPING — How Our Platform Addresses NV030
================================================================================

Our core AI platform for multifunctional coatings (developed for the NSF SBIR
pitch) maps directly to NV030 requirements through three adapted modules:

1. GNN SURROGATE MODELS → COATING MICROSTRUCTURE PREDICTION
   Base technology: Graph neural network property predictors trained on
   molecular graphs with transfer learning from MACE-MP foundation model.
   NV030 adaptation: Apply GNN architecture to coating microstructure graphs
   where nodes = splats/pores/oxide stringers and edges = spatial adjacency.
   Predict process-parameter → coating-property relationships for APS, HVOF,
   and cold spray processes. Multitask heads predict porosity, adhesion,
   hardness, and thermal conductivity simultaneously.

2. GENERATIVE MODELS → PHYSICS-INFORMED DIGITAL TWINS
   Base technology: Junction Tree VAE and diffusion models for molecular
   generation with physics-based constraints.
   NV030 adaptation: Replace molecular generation with neural-ODE digital
   twin of coating deposition physics — in-flight particle dynamics, splat
   formation, residual stress evolution. PINN architecture embeds
   Navier-Stokes, heat transfer, and solid mechanics PDEs as soft constraints.
   Enables 500x faster virtual experiments compared to CFD.

3. BAYESIAN OPTIMIZATION → CLOSED-LOOP PROCESS TUNING
   Base technology: BoTorch qNEHVI multi-objective optimization with
   constraint handling for coating formulations.
   NV030 adaptation: Optimize spray process parameters (spray distance,
   powder feed rate, gas flow, raster velocity) across multiple coating
   quality objectives. Two modes: offline optimization against digital twin,
   and online closed-loop with in-situ sensor feedback.

KEY ADVANTAGE: Our platform uniquely integrates all three capabilities
(microstructure-aware ML, physics-informed digital twins, and closed-loop BO)
in a single system — no existing competitor offers this combination for
thermal spray coating optimization.
