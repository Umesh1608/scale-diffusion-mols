=========================================
RELEVANT NIST RESEARCH AREAS
=========================================

The NIST FY2026 SBIR solicitation covers 8 research areas. Our AI-driven
coating measurement science platform maps to three:

1. ADVANCED MANUFACTURING
   - AI-driven formulation optimization compresses coating development from
     3-5 years to 6-12 months
   - Digital twin models for coating performance prediction enable virtual
     qualification and predictive maintenance
   - Closed-loop AI pipeline (generative design + property prediction + MOBO)
     supports smart manufacturing for coatings
   - Alignment: NIST Manufacturing Extension Partnership (MEP) priorities,
     Smart Manufacturing framework

2. MATERIALS MEASUREMENT SCIENCE
   - Uncertainty-calibrated GNN property predictors as computational reference
     standards for coating characterization
   - AI-assisted EIS measurement protocol to reduce inter-laboratory variance
     from 30-50% to below 10%
   - FAIR-compliant reference database of coating formulation-property
     relationships compatible with NIST MDCS schema
   - Standardized accelerated weathering correlation via chemistry-specific
     digital twin models
   - Alignment: Materials Genome Initiative (MGI), NIST Standard Reference
     Data Program, Materials Data Facility

3. AI/ML FOR MEASUREMENT
   - Machine learning models that improve measurement reproducibility through
     physics-informed priors
   - Anomaly detection for real-time measurement quality assessment (flagging
     instrument drift, protocol deviations)
   - Conformal prediction for calibrated uncertainty quantification on
     property predictions
   - Automated equivalent circuit fitting for EIS using Bayesian information
     criteria
   - Alignment: NIST AI measurement science initiatives, NIST AI Risk
     Management Framework (applied to measurement contexts)

=========================================
SPECIFIC NIST-RELEVANT DELIVERABLES
=========================================

Phase I Deliverables (12 months):
  1. Uncertainty-calibrated GNN models for 4 coating properties (Tg, |Z|,
     lambda_max, adhesion) with conformal prediction intervals benchmarked
     against NIST SRMs
  2. Generative model producing 1,000 novel coating molecules within
     calibrated measurement domains
  3. AI-assisted EIS analysis protocol reducing inter-analyst variance >50%
     on benchmark round-robin datasets
  4. FAIR reference database (5,000+ entries) in NIST MDCS-compatible JSON
  5. Proof-of-concept digital twin for epoxy coating degradation

Phase II Targets:
  - Experimental validation of top AI-generated formulations
  - Formal inter-laboratory round-robin study (3-5 labs)
  - Database expansion to 50,000+ entries
  - NIST-hosted public API for coating property prediction
  - Integration with NIST Materials Data Facility

=========================================
KEY STANDARDS AND PROTOCOLS ADDRESSED
=========================================

  - ASTM D1186  - Dry film thickness measurement
  - ASTM B117   - Salt spray (fog) testing
  - ASTM G154   - Fluorescent UV lamp exposure
  - ASTM G155   - Xenon arc exposure
  - ISO 16773   - EIS on coated/uncoated specimens
  - NIST SRMs   - Polymer thermal properties, spectral data
  - FAIR principles for materials data
  - NIST Materials Data Curation System (MDCS) schema

                    | no measurement protocol standardization

                    | protocols, no FAIR reference databases

                    | coating design + measurement science platform

Our differentiator: Integration of AI-driven molecular design with
measurement science -- calibrated predictions, standardized protocols,
reference databases, and digital twins -- directly aligned with NIST's
unique mission at the intersection of standards and innovation.
