TOPIC DESCRIPTION AND RELEVANCE
---------------------------------
The Army AI/ML Open Topic accepts proposals across six AI/ML sub-fields
relevant to Army modernization:

  1. Computer Vision
  2. Natural Language Processing (NLP)
  3. Autonomous Systems
  4. Predictive Analytics
  5. Materials Informatics
  6. Decision Support Systems

Our proposal maps to sub-fields 4 (Predictive Analytics) and 5 (Materials
Informatics), which are explicitly listed as valid areas for this open topic.

HOW OUR TECHNOLOGY MAPS TO THIS TOPIC
--------------------------------------
Core Technology: AI-Driven Platform for Discovery of Novel Multifunctional
Coating Formulations

The platform integrates three AI/ML capabilities:

  (a) Generative Molecular Design — Junction Tree VAE trained on military-
      relevant molecular fragments to propose novel coating molecules de novo.
      This is a direct application of generative AI to materials science.

  (b) Graph Neural Network Property Prediction — Multitask E(3)-equivariant
      GNNs that predict military-critical coating properties (corrosion
      resistance, IR signature, CARC compliance, mechanical durability)
      from molecular structure. Transfer learning from large-scale materials
      databases addresses the data scarcity problem in military coating R&D.

  (c) Multi-Objective Bayesian Optimization — Constraint-aware optimization
      (BoTorch qNEHVI) that navigates Pareto fronts across 5+ competing
      military performance objectives while enforcing MIL-spec compliance
      as hard constraints.

Army-Specific Applications:

  - CARC (Chemical Agent Resistant Coating) reformulation: AI-driven
    discovery of next-generation CARC formulations per MIL-DTL-53072 and
    MIL-DTL-64159, with improved decontaminant resistance and durability.

  - Infrared Signature Management: Coatings with tailored emissivity across
    SWIR/MWIR/LWIR bands for vehicle thermal signature reduction on
    platforms including Stryker, Bradley, JLTV, and NGCV.

  - Corrosion Protection: AI-optimized anti-corrosion coatings for military
    vehicles and infrastructure operating in extreme environments. DoD
    spends ~$20B/year on corrosion; even incremental improvements have
    massive cost impact.

  - CBRN Protective Coatings: Self-decontaminating surfaces with catalytic
    decomposition of chemical warfare agents (VX, GB, HD).

  - Radar-Absorbing Materials: Polymer matrix materials with controlled
    electromagnetic loss for reduced radar cross-section.

  - Multi-Climate Durability: Coatings performing across -46C to +71C
    per AR 70-38, covering all Army operational environments.

Army Modernization Alignment:

  - Next-Generation Combat Vehicle (NGCV): Coatings for new substrates
  - Future Vertical Lift (FVL): Multi-environment rotorcraft coatings
  - Soldier Lethality: Self-decontaminating equipment coatings
  - Network/C3I: Low-observable coatings for communications infrastructure
  - Sustainment: Corrosion cost reduction across the vehicle fleet

COMPETITIVE POSITIONING
------------------------
Key Differentiators vs. Existing Solutions:

  - Citrine Informatics: General materials AI platform; does not generate
    novel molecules, no military coating focus, no MIL-spec constraints.
  - Uncountable: Formulation management tool; no generative molecular
    design, no multi-objective optimization under military constraints.
  - Schrodinger: Physics-based simulation; computationally expensive,
    not integrated with generative design or Bayesian optimization.

Our platform is the only end-to-end AI pipeline that generates novel
molecules, predicts military-relevant properties, and optimizes
formulations under MIL-spec constraints in a closed loop.
